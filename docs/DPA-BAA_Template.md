## 1. Purpose and Scope
- This Agreement describes the processing of personal data and/or Protected Health Information (PHI) by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the SpeedXToolKit software and related services.
- Activities covered: receipt, storage, transformation, and output of data files (e.g., CSVs containing patient identifiers and test results) as required to provide the Services.

## 2. Roles
- Controller / Covered Entity: determines purposes and means of processing.
- Processor / Business Associate: processes data on Controller's behalf and follows Controller's documented instructions.

## 3. Description of Data and Processing
- Categories of Data Subjects: patients, clinicians, lab staff.
- Categories of Personal Data: identifiers (name, DOB, MRN), contact info, laboratory test results, accession numbers, metadata.
- Special categories / PHI: laboratory test results and other health information; treat as PHI under HIPAA and special category under GDPR.
- Processing operations: ingestion of CSV files, validation, reformatting, aggregation, generation of output files. No automated clinical interpretation is performed.

## 4. Duration of Processing
- Term: from Effective Date until termination/expiration of Services or earlier deletion per Controller instruction.

## 5. Processor Obligations / Security Commitments
Processor shall:
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures (e.g., encryption at rest and in transit, access control, logging, secure coding practices).
- Maintain administrative controls: least-privilege access, role-based access control, staff training on data protection, background checks where applicable.
- Maintain physical and environmental protection for systems storing customer data where applicable.
- Where applicable, maintain ISO 27001 certification (or equivalent) and provide evidence upon request subject to confidentiality.
- Only process data in accordance with Controller's documented instructions.

## 6. Subprocessors
- Controller authorizes Processor to engage subprocessors. Processor will: maintain a current list of subprocessors, carry out due diligence, flow down data protection obligations, and notify Controller of changes.

## 7. International Transfers
- Any transfer of personal data outside the EEA/UK/US will use appropriate safeguards (SCCs, BCRs, or other lawful mechanism) and be documented.

## 8. Data Subject Rights
- Processor will assist Controller in responding to data subject rights requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) where applicable and feasible.

## 9. Breach Notification
- Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay and no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting Controller's data, providing details and mitigation steps.
- For HIPAA-covered PHI breaches, Processor will provide required information to enable Covered Entity to satisfy breach notification obligations.

## 10. Audit and Inspection
- Controller may audit Processor's compliance (e.g., right to request audit reports, SOC2/ISO27001 certificates). Processor will provide reasonable evidence and access under confidentiality protections.

## 11. Data Deletion and Return
- Upon termination, Processor will, at Controller's choice, return or securely delete all personal data and certify deletion within a defined timeframe (e.g., 30 days). Backups will be purged in accordance with Processor retention policies.

## 12. Liability and Indemnity
- Allocate liability consistent with applicable law. Processor liability limited to willful misconduct or gross negligence; indemnities for breach of agreement or negligent security failures.
